Key Definitions
Constant: No variable. 7 = 7·x⁰ = 7·1 = 7. It never changes.
Variable: The unknown letter. Could be x, y, n, etc.
Coefficient: The number in front. If none shows, the coefficient is 1 (hidden).
Degree: The highest exponent in the polynomial. In 3x² + 5x the degree is 2.
Leading Coefficient: The coefficient of the highest-degree term. In 5x³ + 2x + 1, the leading coefficient is 5.
x⁰ = 1: Any variable to the power of 0 equals 1, which is why constants have degree 0.

6 Adding Polynomials

To add polynomials: find like terms → group them → add the coefficients. The variable and exponent stay the same.

1
Find like terms — same variable and same exponent
2
Group like terms — put them together in parentheses
3
Add the coefficients — keep the variable and exponent unchanged
Example — Addition
Problem(3x² + 5x + 2) + (x² − 3x + 7)
Step 1: Find3x² and x² | 5x and −3x | 2 and 7
Step 2: Group(3x² + x²) + (5x − 3x) + (2 + 7)
Step 3: Add4x² + 2x + 9
Answer4x² + 2x + 9
7 Subtracting Polynomials

Subtraction is the same as addition after you distribute the negative sign. Every sign in the second polynomial flips.

Most common mistake — forgetting to flip ALL signs:
−(2x² − 4x + 6) = −2x² + 4x − 6  ✅ every sign flips
−(2x² − 4x + 6) = −2x² − 4x + 6  ❌ wrong
1
Distribute the − to every term in the second polynomial — flip every sign
2
Find like terms in the new combined expression
3
Group and combine exactly like addition
Example — Subtraction
Problem(5x² + 3x − 1) − (2x² − 4x + 6)
Distribute −5x² + 3x − 1  − 2x² + 4x − 6
Step 2: Group(5x² − 2x²) + (3x + 4x) + (−1 − 6)
Step 3: Add3x² + 7x + (−7)
Answer3x² + 7x − 7
